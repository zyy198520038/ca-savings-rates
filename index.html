What is a GIC?
A Guaranteed Investment Certificate (GIC) is a deposit you lock in at a fixed interest rate for a set term. Your principal is guaranteed.
⚖️
GIC vs HISA
HISA is flexible but rate can change. GIC locks your money but offers a higher guaranteed rate. Use HISA for emergency funds; GIC for money you won't need for 1–5 years.
🏦
Registered vs non-registered
Non-registered: interest is taxable. Inside TFSA: tax-free growth. Inside RRSP: tax-deferred. If you have TFSA room, a TFSA GIC is usually the better choice.
🛡️
CDIC insured?
Yes — GICs at CDIC member banks are insured up to $100,000 per depositor per category for terms up to 5 years. Credit union GICs are covered by provincial insurers (e.g. CUDIC in BC).
